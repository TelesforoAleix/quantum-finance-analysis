## Key Findings

- [supported] In two empirical AAPL European call option case studies, both Iterative Amplitude Estimation (IAE) and Maximum Likelihood Amplitude Estimation (MLAE) returned an expected payoff of 0.00 USD, while classical benchmarks produced positive expected payoffs. — [[2026_Barbaresco_QuantumAmplitudeEstimation]]
- [supported] For the 2023–2024 scenario, the classical exact benchmark estimated a payoff of 17.5987 USD and Monte Carlo estimated 17.8259 USD, whereas both IAE and MLAE estimated 0.0000 USD. — [[2026_Barbaresco_QuantumAmplitudeEstimation]]
- [supported] For the 2022–2023 scenario, the classical exact benchmark estimated a payoff of 17.0597 USD and Monte Carlo estimated 17.2799 USD, whereas both IAE and MLAE estimated 0.0000 USD. — [[2026_Barbaresco_QuantumAmplitudeEstimation]]
- [supported] Monte Carlo results with 100,000 samples were within about 1% of the classical exact discretized benchmark in both scenarios. — [[2026_Barbaresco_QuantumAmplitudeEstimation]]
- [supported] Quantum-estimated asset prices were systematically lower than classical estimates: 185.3681 USD vs 198.0063 USD exact (and 198.1746 USD MC) in 2023–2024, and about 179.69 USD vs 191.9414 USD exact (and 192.1046 USD MC) in 2022–2023. — [[2026_Barbaresco_QuantumAmplitudeEstimation]]
- [supported] The paper attributes the quantum underestimation to coarse state encoding with 6 uncertainty qubits (64 bins), which failed to resolve low-probability in-the-money tail events that materially contribute to expected payoff. — [[2026_Barbaresco_QuantumAmplitudeEstimation]]
- [supported] The experiments were conducted in a simulated quantum environment using Qiskit rather than on real quantum hardware. — [[2026_Barbaresco_QuantumAmplitudeEstimation]]
- [supported] Even under noiseless simulation and expressive compiled circuits, the main limitation was the encoded probability landscape rather than circuit depth; the paper reports transpiled circuit sizes of about 172,000 gates for IAE and 12,600 gates for MLAE. — [[2026_Barbaresco_QuantumAmplitudeEstimation]]
- [supported] Both IAE and MLAE matched the realized market outcome of zero payoff in the tested cases, suggesting usefulness for binary threshold or event-detection tasks under coarse resolution. — [[2026_Barbaresco_QuantumAmplitudeEstimation]]
- [supported] In a numerical example for a simple classical 1D ODE without shot noise, the most resource-efficient Runge-Kutta method among orders 1-10 was order 4. — [[2026_Dechant_ErrorResourceEstimates]]
- [supported] In that noiseless ODE example, all tested Runge-Kutta methods of order 2 through 10 were more cost-efficient than Euler order 1 under the chosen parameter setting. — [[2026_Dechant_ErrorResourceEstimates]]
- [supported] For the noiseless ODE benchmark with target error 0.001, the order-4 Runge-Kutta method reduced estimated cost by about 2.22 x 10^3 relative to Euler. — [[2026_Dechant_ErrorResourceEstimates]]
- [supported] In the option-pricing Black-Scholes PDE case studied with the variational algorithm and the authors' default parameter estimates, the most resource-efficient Runge-Kutta method was order 2. — [[2026_Dechant_ErrorResourceEstimates]]
- [supported] For the option-pricing case with target error 0.001 and default estimates, the order-2 method reduced estimated total circuit evaluations by a factor of 13.18 relative to Euler. — [[2026_Dechant_ErrorResourceEstimates]]
- [supported] For the option-pricing case with target error 0.001 and default estimates, the authors estimate the algorithm would require about 1.62 x 10^28 total quantum circuit evaluations, making it impractical on current hardware. — [[2026_Dechant_ErrorResourceEstimates]]
- [supported] For the same option-pricing setup, the estimated measurements per circuit/function evaluation were about 3.87 x 10^22, corresponding to roughly classical machine precision in matrix/vector entries. — [[2026_Dechant_ErrorResourceEstimates]]
- [supported] The paper argues from its numerical estimates that matrix inversion in the variational update is the main driver of severe shot-noise amplification and resource overhead. — [[2026_Dechant_ErrorResourceEstimates]]
- [supported] In an alternative tuned parameter scenario for the option-pricing-style analysis, the best method became order 4, with an estimated savings factor of about 2.56 x 10^3 over Euler, though still far beyond feasible hardware limits. — [[2026_Dechant_ErrorResourceEstimates]]
- [supported] The review identifies QAOA, VQE, QNNs, QSVMs, Grover-based methods, QPCA, and quantum k-means as the main QML approaches studied for optimization across finance, logistics, AI, and chemistry. — [[2026_Nawaz_ExploringQuantumMachine]]
- [supported] Across the reviewed literature, the paper finds a broad theme that QML methods show potential for exploring large solution spaces, faster convergence, and improved optimization performance relative to some classical approaches, but this is not established as a general empirical superiority. — [[2026_Nawaz_ExploringQuantumMachine]]

## Open Questions

- How many uncertainty qubits are needed before QAE-based option pricing begins to align with classical expected-payoff estimates? — [[2026_Barbaresco_QuantumAmplitudeEstimation]]
- Can nonuniform or adaptive binning around the strike resolve low-probability but financially significant tail events more effectively than uniform discretization? — [[2026_Barbaresco_QuantumAmplitudeEstimation]]
- Would alternative state-preparation or payoff-encoding strategies allow IAE and MLAE to recover nonzero expected payoffs under realistic qubit budgets? — [[2026_Barbaresco_QuantumAmplitudeEstimation]]
- How robust are IAE and MLAE to real hardware noise when applied to option pricing circuits of this size? — [[2026_Barbaresco_QuantumAmplitudeEstimation]]
- Can QAE methods provide practical advantage for expectation estimation in finance before fault-tolerant quantum hardware becomes available? — [[2026_Barbaresco_QuantumAmplitudeEstimation]]
- Are QAE methods more suitable for binary event detection or threshold classification than for full expectation estimation in near-term financial applications? — [[2026_Barbaresco_QuantumAmplitudeEstimation]]
- How sensitive are the results to modeling assumptions such as log-normal returns, volatility clipping, and the imposed bullish return shift? — [[2026_Barbaresco_QuantumAmplitudeEstimation]]
- Would increasing objective qubits or changing payoff approximation schemes materially improve pricing accuracy? — [[2026_Barbaresco_QuantumAmplitudeEstimation]]
- How do the findings extend to other assets, maturities, strikes, and option classes such as puts, American options, or path-dependent derivatives? — [[2026_Barbaresco_QuantumAmplitudeEstimation]]
- What is the trade-off between circuit depth, qubit count, and estimation accuracy for financially relevant tail-sensitive payoffs? — [[2026_Barbaresco_QuantumAmplitudeEstimation]]

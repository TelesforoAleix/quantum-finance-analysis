## Abstract summary
This paper introduces CNN-CVaR-QAOA, a hybrid approach combining convolutional neural networks (CNNs) and conditional value at risk (CVaR) to improve the quantum approximate optimization algorithm (QAOA) for combinatorial problems. The study focuses on optimizing QAOA parameters by replacing traditional loss functions with CVaR and leveraging CNNs for variational parameter prediction, aiming to enhance performance on near-term quantum devices. The method is validated through experiments on random graphs, demonstrating its potential to advance quantum optimization techniques.
## Methodology
The paper presents an empirical study introducing a novel combinatorial optimization strategy, CNN-CVaR-QAOA, which integrates a convolutional neural network (CNN) with conditional value at risk (CVaR) to optimize quantum approximate optimization algorithm (QAOA) circuits. The research focuses on enhancing QAOA performance by replacing the traditional loss function with CVaR and leveraging CNN for variational quantum parameter optimization. The methodology involves training a CNN model to predict optimal QAOA parameters for deeper circuits based on initial parameters generated by the QN-SPSA optimizer. The CVaR aggregation function is used to smooth the objective function, prioritizing better observed outcomes. The study evaluates the proposed method on Max-Cut problem instances using Erdos–Renyi random graphs, comparing its performance against baseline methods such as random initialization (RI-QAOA), interpolation (INTERP-QAOA), CNN-QAOA, and CVaR-QAOA. The approximation ratio is used as the primary evaluation metric to assess the effectiveness of the CNN-CVaR-QAOA approach.

**Algorithms used:** QAOA, CNN-CVaR-QAOA
**Frameworks:** Qiskit

**Experimental setup:** Experiments were conducted using the noiseless state vector simulator of the Qiskit platform. The quantum circuit measurements were set to 1024 to ensure data adequacy and reliability. The CNN model was trained with a batch size of 6 and 50 epochs, using the Adam optimizer with a learning rate of 0.0001 and mean squared error (MSE) as the loss function. The study utilized Erdos–Renyi random graphs generated via the Python NetworkX package for Max-Cut problem instances.

**Dataset:** Erdos–Renyi random graphs of varying sizes (6 to 16 nodes), node degrees (3 to 8), and edge probabilities (0.5 to 0.7) were used for the Max-Cut problem instances. Multiple instances were generated for each configuration to derive statistical outcomes.
## Findings

- [supported] CNN-CVaR-QAOA (α = 0.5) improves approximation ratios by 0.06–0.2 over competing methods at circuit depths p=2 and p=3.
- [supported] CNN-CVaR-QAOA converges to optimal solutions at shallower circuit depths (e.g., p=3 for 8-node graphs, p=4 for 12-node graphs) compared to other methods, which require deeper circuits (e.g., p=7).

**Results summary:** The paper empirically demonstrates that CNN-CVaR-QAOA outperforms four baseline methods (RI-QAOA, INTERP-QAOA, CNN-QAOA, CVaR-QAOA) in solving the Max-Cut problem on Erdos–Renyi random graphs. Key results include higher approximation ratios (improvements of 0.06–0.2) and faster convergence to optimal solutions at shallower circuit depths (e.g., p=3 vs. p=7 for 12-node graphs). The study also quantifies the impact of the CVaR parameter α, showing that lower values (e.g., 0.1) enhance performance by smoothing the objective function and expanding the range of optimal variational parameters. All results are derived from noiseless state vector simulations using Qiskit, with 1024 measurements per circuit.

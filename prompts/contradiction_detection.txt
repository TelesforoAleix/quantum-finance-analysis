Your task: Given claims extracted from multiple papers on the same topic, identify where papers directly contradict each other. Compare claims pairwise across all papers provided.

**Topic being analyzed:** {topic}

**Severity levels:**

- high: Direct contradiction — Paper A claims X, Paper B claims not-X, both citing evidence
- medium: Tension — papers make claims that are hard to reconcile but do not directly contradict
- low: Different emphasis — papers frame the same issue differently, suggesting different conclusions

**Instructions:**

1. Compare claims pairwise across all papers below.
2. Focus on factual contradictions about quantum advantage, performance, scalability, and feasibility.
3. Do NOT flag as contradictions:
   - Different experimental setups comparing different things (e.g. different qubit counts, different algorithms)
   - Papers studying different sub-problems within the topic
   - Claims at different levels of generality (e.g. one paper discusses theory, another discusses a specific implementation)
4. For each contradiction found, explain WHY it is a contradiction — what specifically is incompatible between the two claims.
5. If no contradictions exist among the papers, return an empty contradictions list.

**Papers and their claims:**

{paper_claims}

**Output format:**

Return a JSON object with the topic and a list of contradictions. Use descriptive short labels for "type" values: "scalability", "quantum-advantage", "noise-resilience", "performance", "feasibility", "methodology", etc.
